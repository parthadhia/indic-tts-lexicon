# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want this project to be a respectful, useful, and inclusive place for contributors across languages, regions, and communities.

Participants are expected to:

- be respectful in technical and cultural discussions
- assume good intent while still being precise about disagreements
- welcome corrections on pronunciation, transliteration, and regional nuance
- avoid dismissive language about people, communities, accents, or traditions

## Unacceptable Behavior

- harassment or personal attacks
- insulting language about linguistic, regional, caste, religious, or cultural identity
- deliberate misrepresentation of community pronunciation practices
- bad-faith edits or repeated disregard for documented review guidance

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove or reject content that violates this code of conduct and may limit participation when needed to protect the project and its contributors.
